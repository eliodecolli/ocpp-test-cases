E06 - Stop Transaction options
Test Cases
================================================================================

TC-E06-001
Title: Stop transaction when parking bay occupancy detector no longer detects EV
Requirement: E06.FR.01
Steps:
  1. Ensure a transaction is ongoing at the Charging Station.
  2. Configure TxStopPoint to contain: ParkingBayOccupancy.
  3. Cause the parking bay occupancy detector to stop detecting the EV (EV departs from bay).
Expected Result:
  The Charging Station stops the transaction.
  The Charging Station sends a TransactionEventRequest to the CSMS with eventType = Ended
  and triggerReason = EVDeparted and stoppedReason = Local.
  The CSMS responds with a TransactionEventResponse confirming receipt.
  The transaction is no longer active on the Charging Station.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-E06-002
Title: Stop transaction when EV connection is lost (EVConnected stop point)
Requirement: E06.FR.02
Steps:
  1. Ensure a transaction is ongoing at the Charging Station.
  2. Configure TxStopPoint to contain: EVConnected.
  3. Unplug the charging cable, severing communication between the Charging Station and the EV.
  4. If the cable was unplugged on the Charging Station side, observe whether a StatusNotificationRequest is sent to the CSMS indicating the connector is Available.
Expected Result:
  The Charging Station stops the transaction.
  The Charging Station sends a TransactionEventRequest to the CSMS with eventType = Ended,
  chargingState = idle, triggerReason = EVCommunicationLost, and stoppedReason = EVDisconnected.
  The CSMS responds with a TransactionEventResponse confirming receipt.
  If the cable was unplugged on the Charging Station side, a StatusNotificationRequest indicating
  the connector is Available is sent before or alongside the TransactionEventRequest.
  The transaction is no longer active on the Charging Station.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-E06-003
Title: Stop transaction when EV driver presents valid IdToken to stop charging (driver-authorized stop)
Requirement: E06.FR.03
Steps:
  1. Ensure a transaction is ongoing at the Charging Station.
  2. Configure TxStopPoint to contain: Authorized.
  3. The EV driver presents a valid IdToken at the Charging Station to authorize stopping the transaction.
Expected Result:
  The Charging Station determines the EV driver is authorized to stop the transaction.
  The Charging Station stops the transaction.
  The Charging Station sends a TransactionEventRequest to the CSMS with eventType = Ended
  and triggerReason = StopAuthorized.
  The TransactionEventRequest includes the idToken used to stop (e.g., idToken.id = the presented token)
  and stoppedReason = Local (or stoppedReason may be omitted per E06.FR.09).
  The CSMS responds with a TransactionEventResponse.
  The transaction is no longer active on the Charging Station.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-E06-004
Title: Stop transaction when CSMS returns non-valid idTokenInfo in TransactionEventResponse (StopTxOnInvalidId = true)
Requirement: E06.FR.04
Steps:
  1. Ensure a transaction is ongoing at the Charging Station.
  2. Configure TxStopPoint to contain: Authorized.
  3. Configure StopTxOnInvalidId = true on the Charging Station.
  4. The Charging Station sends a TransactionEventRequest to the CSMS during the transaction.
  5. The CSMS responds with a TransactionEventResponse containing idTokenInfo.status with a value
     other than Accepted (e.g., Blocked, Invalid, or Expired).
Expected Result:
  The Charging Station stops the energy offer immediately upon receiving the non-valid idTokenInfo.
  The Charging Station sends a TransactionEventRequest to the CSMS with eventType = Ended,
  triggerReason = Deauthorized, and stoppedReason = DeAuthorized.
  The CSMS responds with a TransactionEventResponse.
  The transaction is no longer active on the Charging Station.

TC-E06-004b
Title: Updated transaction state (not ended) when CSMS returns non-valid idTokenInfo and StopTxOnInvalidId = false
Requirement: E06.FR.04
Steps:
  1. Ensure a transaction is ongoing at the Charging Station.
  2. Configure TxStopPoint to contain: Authorized.
  3. Configure StopTxOnInvalidId = false on the Charging Station.
  4. The Charging Station sends a TransactionEventRequest to the CSMS during the transaction.
  5. The CSMS responds with a TransactionEventResponse containing idTokenInfo.status with a value
     other than Accepted (e.g., Blocked, Invalid, or Expired).
Expected Result:
  The Charging Station stops the energy offer.
  The Charging Station sends a TransactionEventRequest to the CSMS with eventType = Updated
  and triggerReason = ChargingStateChanged (transaction is NOT ended in this branch).
  The CSMS responds with a TransactionEventResponse.
  The transaction remains active (not ended) on the Charging Station.

TC-E06-008
Title: stoppedReason is included when transaction is not ended by the EV driver locally
Requirement: E06.FR.08
Steps:
  1. Ensure a transaction is ongoing at the Charging Station.
  2. Trigger a stop condition that is NOT caused by the EV driver presenting an IdToken locally
     (e.g., EVCommunicationLost, EVDeparted, AbnormalCondition, or deauthorization from CSMS).
  3. Observe the TransactionEventRequest (eventType = Ended) sent by the Charging Station.
Expected Result:
  The TransactionEventRequest (eventType = Ended) includes the stoppedReason field.
  The stoppedReason value is set to the appropriate reason for the stop condition
  as described in reasonEnumType (e.g., EVDisconnected, Local, DeAuthorized, etc.).
  The stoppedReason field is not omitted or empty.

TC-E06-009
Title: stoppedReason may be omitted when transaction is ended by EV driver locally
Requirement: E06.FR.09
Steps:
  1. Ensure a transaction is ongoing at the Charging Station.
  2. The EV driver presents an IdToken at the Charging Station to stop the transaction locally.
  3. Observe the TransactionEventRequest (eventType = Ended) sent by the Charging Station.
Expected Result:
  The Charging Station sends a TransactionEventRequest with eventType = Ended.
  The stoppedReason field MAY be omitted from the TransactionEventRequest.
  If the stoppedReason field is omitted, the CSMS interprets the reason as Local.
  If the stoppedReason field is present, it is set to Local.
  The CSMS responds with a TransactionEventResponse.

TC-E06-010
Title: Charging cable is unlocked upon normal transaction termination
Requirement: E06.FR.10
Steps:
  1. Ensure a transaction is ongoing at the Charging Station with a non-permanently-attached cable.
  2. End the transaction through any normal termination path (e.g., EV driver presents IdToken to stop).
  3. Observe the behavior of the cable lock mechanism on the Charging Station.
Expected Result:
  Upon normal transaction termination, the Charging Station unlocks the cable.
  The connector lock is released, allowing the cable to be physically unplugged.
  This unlock action is reflected in the sequence (e.g., as shown in S7, "unlock connector" step
  occurs after user authorization is successful and before or alongside the TransactionEventRequest).

TC-E06-012
Title: Meter data may be dropped when Charging Station is low on memory
Requirement: E06.FR.12
Steps:
  1. Configure the Charging Station to send meter data in the TransactionEventRequest
     (eventType = Ended) per E06.FR.11.
  2. Simulate a low-memory condition on the Charging Station.
  3. Trigger a normal transaction stop.
  4. Observe the TransactionEventRequest (eventType = Ended) sent to the CSMS.
Expected Result:
  The Charging Station MAY drop meter data from the meterValue field in the
  TransactionEventRequest (eventType = Ended) due to low memory.
  The TransactionEventRequest is still sent with eventType = Ended.
  If meter data is dropped, the remaining meter values still conform to E06.FR.13
  (intermediate values are dropped first).

TC-E06-013
Title: When dropping meter data, intermediate values are dropped first (not from start or end)
Requirement: E06.FR.13
Steps:
  1. Configure the Charging Station to send meter data in the TransactionEventRequest
     (eventType = Ended) with multiple meter value samples collected during the transaction.
  2. Simulate a low-memory condition such that the Charging Station must drop some meter values.
  3. Trigger a normal transaction stop.
  4. Observe the meterValue list in the TransactionEventRequest (eventType = Ended).
Expected Result:
  The Charging Station drops intermediate meter values first (i.e., odd-indexed intermediate
  values such as the 1st, 3rd, 5th values in the sequence are removed first).
  The Charging Station does NOT drop values from the start of the list (i.e., the first recorded
  value is preserved as long as possible).
  The Charging Station does NOT stop adding new values to the end of the list (i.e., the final
  recorded value is preserved as long as possible).
  The remaining meter values in the TransactionEventRequest reflect this dropping strategy.

TC-E06-014
Title: seqNo field is correctly set in TransactionEventRequest
Requirement: E06.FR.14
Steps:
  1. Ensure a transaction is ongoing with at least one prior TransactionEventRequest already sent
     (establishing an existing seqNo sequence for this transaction).
  2. Trigger a stop condition so that a TransactionEventRequest (eventType = Ended) must be created.
  3. Observe the seqNo field in the TransactionEventRequest (eventType = Ended).
Expected Result:
  The TransactionEventRequest (eventType = Ended) contains a seqNo field.
  The seqNo value is set according to the Sequence Number Generation rules (i.e., it is one
  greater than the previous seqNo sent for the same transaction, e.g., seqNo = N + 1 where N
  was the seqNo of the last TransactionEventRequest for this transaction).
  The seqNo is not repeated or out of order relative to previous messages in the same transaction.

TC-E06-015
Title: triggerReason field is set in every TransactionEventRequest
Requirement: E06.FR.15
Steps:
  1. Trigger a transaction stop via each of the supported stop conditions in turn:
     a. Parking bay occupancy detector no longer detects the EV.
     b. EV communication is lost (cable unplugged).
     c. EV driver presents IdToken to stop transaction.
     d. CSMS returns non-valid idTokenInfo.
     e. Energy transfer stops.
     f. Abnormal error or fault condition.
  2. For each stop condition, observe the TransactionEventRequest sent to the CSMS.
Expected Result:
  Every TransactionEventRequest sent by the Charging Station includes the triggerReason field.
  The triggerReason value corresponds to the actual cause of the event as described in
  TriggerReasonEnumType:
    - EVDeparted when the parking bay detector no longer detects the EV (S1).
    - EVCommunicationLost when the cable is unplugged (S2).
    - StopAuthorized when the EV driver presents an IdToken to stop (S7).
    - Deauthorized when the CSMS returns a non-valid idTokenInfo and StopTxOnInvalidId = true (S3).
    - AbnormalCondition when an abnormal error or fault condition triggers the stop (FR.16).
  The field is never absent from any TransactionEventRequest.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-E06-016
Title: Transaction stopped by abnormal error or fault sends AbnormalCondition triggerReason
Requirement: E06.FR.16
Steps:
  1. Ensure a transaction is ongoing at the Charging Station.
  2. Introduce or simulate an abnormal error or fault condition at the Charging Station
     (e.g., an internal fault that forces the transaction to terminate).
  3. Observe the TransactionEventRequest sent by the Charging Station to the CSMS.
Expected Result:
  The Charging Station sends a TransactionEventRequest with eventType = Ended
  and triggerReason = AbnormalCondition.
  The CSMS receives this message and responds with a TransactionEventResponse.
  The transaction is no longer active on the Charging Station.
  The stoppedReason field is included in the TransactionEventRequest (per E06.FR.08,
  since the transaction was not ended by the EV driver locally).
