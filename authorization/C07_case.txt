C07 - Authorization using Contract Certificates
Test Cases
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The C07 use case describes how an Electric Vehicle (EV) is authorized to charge
at a Charging Station using a Contract Certificate (issued as part of the ISO
15118 Plug & Charge mechanism). The Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest
to the Charge Station Management System (CSMS), which validates the contract
certificate and its chain using OCSP (Online Certificate Status Protocol) data.
The CSMS replies with an authorization status, and charging only begins after
successful authorization.

Actors involved: EV, Charging Station, CSMS, OCSP server.

Key configuration flags referenced:
  - CentralContractValidationAllowed: allows the CSMS to validate the
    certificate chain when the Charging Station lacks the root certificate.
  - ContractValidationOffline: controls whether offline validation is attempted.
  - LocalAuthorizeOffline: controls whether the Local Authorization List or
    Cache is consulted during offline validation.

================================================================================

TEST CASE 1
-----------
Title: Successful authorization with valid contract certificate (online)

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is online, the EV presents a valid contract certificate,
  and the CSMS successfully validates it via OCSP.

Steps:
  1. Connect an EV to the Charging Station.
  2. The EV provides its contract certificate and certificate chain to the
     Charging Station via ISO 15118 communication.
  3. The Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS containing:
       - The eMAID (e-Mobility Account Identifier) extracted from the contract
         certificate.
       - The ISO 15118 certificate hash data (iso15118CertificateHashData)
         needed for the OCSP request, covering the contract certificate and its
         chain.
  4. The CSMS performs OCSP validation (real-time or cached) using the provided
     hash data and determines the certificate is valid and not revoked.
  5. The CSMS sends an AuthorizeResponse with an authorization status of
     "Accepted".
  6. The Charging Station starts the charging session.

Expected Result:
  - The AuthorizeRequest contains both the eMAID and the OCSP hash data.
  - The CSMS responds with AuthorizeResponse indicating "Accepted".
  - The Charging Station starts charging for the EV.

TEST CASE 2
-----------
Title: Authorization rejected due to revoked contract certificate

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is online, but the OCSP check reveals the contract
  certificate has been revoked.

Steps:
  1. Connect an EV to the Charging Station.
  2. The EV provides a contract certificate that is revoked in the OCSP
     infrastructure.
  3. The Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS with the eMAID
     and the iso15118CertificateHashData.
  4. The CSMS performs the OCSP check and discovers the certificate has been
     revoked.
  5. The CSMS sends an AuthorizeResponse with an authorization status indicating
     rejection (e.g., "Invalid" or a revocation-related reason).
  6. The Charging Station does not start the charging session.

Expected Result:
  - The AuthorizeResponse contains a non-acceptance status reflecting revocation.
  - The Charging Station denies charging to the EV.

================================================================================

TEST CASE 3
-----------
Title: Authorization rejected due to expired contract certificate

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is online, but the contract certificate presented by the
  EV has passed its expiry date.

Steps:
  1. Connect an EV to the Charging Station.
  2. The EV provides an expired contract certificate.
  3. The Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS with the eMAID
     and the iso15118CertificateHashData.
  4. The CSMS validates the certificate and determines it is expired.
  5. The CSMS sends an AuthorizeResponse with a rejection status (e.g.,
     "Invalid" or an expiry-related reason).
  6. The Charging Station does not start the charging session.

Expected Result:
  - The AuthorizeResponse indicates rejection due to invalid/expired certificate.
  - The Charging Station refuses to charge the EV.

TEST CASE 4
-----------
Title: AuthorizeRequest must include eMAID and OCSP hash data

Scenario:
  Verify that the AuthorizeRequest message sent by the Charging Station is
  properly formed, containing both the eMAID and the certificate hash data
  required for the OCSP request (requirement C07.FR.02).

Steps:
  1. Connect an EV to the Charging Station (Charging Station is online).
  2. The EV provides its contract certificate and certificate chain.
  3. Capture the AuthorizeRequest message sent by the Charging Station to the
     CSMS.
  4. Inspect the captured message for the presence of:
       - The eMAID field populated with the EV's e-Mobility Account Identifier.
       - The iso15118CertificateHashData field containing hash data for the
         contract certificate and the full certificate chain.

Expected Result:
  - The AuthorizeRequest contains a non-empty eMAID.
  - The AuthorizeRequest contains iso15118CertificateHashData covering both the
    contract certificate and the certificate chain.
  - No required field is absent or empty.

================================================================================

TEST CASE 5
-----------
Title: CSMS always responds to an AuthorizeRequest

Scenario:
  Verify that the CSMS never silently ignores an AuthorizeRequest — it always
  sends back an AuthorizeResponse with an explicit status (requirement C07.FR.04).

Steps:
  1. Connect an EV to the Charging Station (Charging Station is online).
  2. The EV provides its contract certificate.
  3. The Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS.
  4. Observe the CSMS behavior and capture its response.

Expected Result:
  - The CSMS sends an AuthorizeResponse for every received AuthorizeRequest.
  - The AuthorizeResponse always contains an authorization status value
    (accepted or a specific reason for rejection).
  - The CSMS never times out without sending a response.

TEST CASE 6
-----------
Title: Charging Station passes full certificate chain when it lacks root
       certificate and CentralContractValidationAllowed is true

Scenario:
  The Charging Station does not have the root certificate needed to validate the
  contract certificate locally. The configuration flag
  CentralContractValidationAllowed is set to true, so the Charging Station
  delegates full validation to the CSMS by sending the certificate chain
  (requirement C07.FR.06).

Steps:
  1. Configure the Charging Station without the relevant root certificate.
  2. Set CentralContractValidationAllowed to true on the Charging Station.
  3. Connect an EV and have it present a contract certificate.
  4. The Charging Station attempts to validate the contract certificate locally
     but cannot because the root certificate is missing.
  5. Capture the AuthorizeRequest sent to the CSMS.
  6. Inspect the AuthorizeRequest for the presence of the full certificate chain
     in PEM format within the certificate attribute.
  7. The CSMS validates the chain and responds with an AuthorizeResponse.

Expected Result:
  - The AuthorizeRequest includes the complete contract certificate chain in PEM
    format in the certificate attribute.
  - The CSMS is able to perform validation using the provided chain.
  - The CSMS responds with the appropriate authorization status.

================================================================================

TEST CASE 7
-----------
Title: Charging Station does NOT pass certificate chain when
       CentralContractValidationAllowed is false and root certificate is missing

Scenario:
  The Charging Station lacks the root certificate but CentralContractValidationAllowed
  is false. The Charging Station should not attempt to pass the certificate chain
  to the CSMS for central validation.

Steps:
  1. Configure the Charging Station without the relevant root certificate.
  2. Set CentralContractValidationAllowed to false on the Charging Station.
  3. Connect an EV and have it present a contract certificate.
  4. Observe the Charging Station behavior.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station does not include the certificate chain (PEM) in the
    AuthorizeRequest (or may deny the session locally).
  - Central certificate validation is not performed.
  - Charging is not started (since local validation cannot succeed without the
    root certificate).

TEST CASE 8
-----------
Title: Charging Station offline, ContractValidationOffline is false — charging
       not allowed

Scenario:
  The Charging Station has lost its connection to the CSMS. The configuration
  flag ContractValidationOffline is set to false, meaning offline contract
  validation is not permitted (requirement C07.FR.07).

Steps:
  1. Disconnect the Charging Station from the CSMS (simulate offline state).
  2. Set ContractValidationOffline to false on the Charging Station.
  3. Connect an EV and have it present a valid contract certificate.
  4. Observe the Charging Station behavior.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station does not attempt local validation.
  - The Charging Station does not start a charging session.
  - The EV is denied charging until the Charging Station comes back online.

================================================================================

TEST CASE 9
-----------
Title: Charging Station offline, ContractValidationOffline is true — local
       validation attempted

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is offline and ContractValidationOffline is true. The
  Charging Station should try to validate the contract certificate locally
  (requirement C07.FR.08).

Steps:
  1. Disconnect the Charging Station from the CSMS (simulate offline state).
  2. Set ContractValidationOffline to true on the Charging Station.
  3. Connect an EV and have it present a contract certificate.
  4. Observe the Charging Station behavior.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station does not send an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS (since
    it is offline).
  - The Charging Station attempts to validate the contract certificate using its
    locally available certificates and OCSP cache.

TEST CASE 10
------------
Title: Offline validation success — eMAID found in Local Authorization List,
       LocalAuthorizeOffline true

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is offline, ContractValidationOffline is true, the
  contract certificate is locally valid, LocalAuthorizeOffline is true, and the
  eMAID is present in the Local Authorization List (requirements C07.FR.09,
  C07.FR.10).

Steps:
  1. Disconnect the Charging Station from the CSMS.
  2. Set ContractValidationOffline to true.
  3. Set LocalAuthorizeOffline to true.
  4. Pre-populate the Local Authorization List with the EV's eMAID, marked as
     accepted.
  5. Ensure the Charging Station has the necessary certificates to validate the
     contract certificate locally.
  6. Connect an EV and have it present the contract certificate.
  7. The Charging Station validates the contract certificate locally (valid).
  8. The Charging Station looks up the eMAID in the Local Authorization List and
     finds it with an "Accepted" status.
  9. Observe the Charging Station behavior.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station successfully validates the certificate locally.
  - The eMAID is found in the Local Authorization List with an accepted status.
  - The Charging Station starts the charging session without contacting the CSMS.

TEST CASE 11
------------
Title: Offline validation — eMAID found in Authorization Cache (not Local
       Authorization List)

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is offline, ContractValidationOffline is true, the
  contract certificate is locally valid, LocalAuthorizeOffline is true, and the
  eMAID is not in the Local Authorization List but is found in the Authorization
  Cache (requirement C07.FR.09 covers both sources).

Steps:
  1. Disconnect the Charging Station from the CSMS.
  2. Set ContractValidationOffline to true.
  3. Set LocalAuthorizeOffline to true.
  4. Ensure the Local Authorization List does NOT contain the EV's eMAID.
  5. Ensure the Authorization Cache contains the EV's eMAID with an accepted
     status from a previous online session.
  6. Ensure the Charging Station has the necessary certificates for local
     validation.
  7. Connect an EV and have it present the contract certificate.
  8. The Charging Station validates the certificate locally (valid).
  9. The Charging Station looks up the eMAID — not in Local Authorization List,
     but found in Authorization Cache.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station finds the eMAID in the Authorization Cache.
  - The Charging Station starts the charging session based on the cached
    authorization.

TEST CASE 12
------------
Title: Offline validation — eMAID not found in Local Authorization List or Cache

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is offline, ContractValidationOffline is true, the
  contract certificate is locally valid, LocalAuthorizeOffline is true, but the
  eMAID is not found in either the Local Authorization List or the Authorization
  Cache.

Steps:
  1. Disconnect the Charging Station from the CSMS.
  2. Set ContractValidationOffline to true.
  3. Set LocalAuthorizeOffline to true.
  4. Ensure neither the Local Authorization List nor the Authorization Cache
     contains the EV's eMAID.
  5. Connect an EV and have it present the contract certificate.
  6. The Charging Station validates the certificate locally (valid).
  7. The Charging Station looks up the eMAID in the Local Authorization List and
     Authorization Cache — not found in either.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station cannot authorize the EV without a matching entry.
  - Charging is denied until the Charging Station can reach the CSMS or the
     eMAID is added to the local data.

TEST CASE 13
------------
Title: Offline validation — contract certificate is locally invalid

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is offline, ContractValidationOffline is true, but the
  local validation of the contract certificate fails (e.g., the certificate is
  expired or has an invalid signature as determined locally).

Steps:
  1. Disconnect the Charging Station from the CSMS.
  2. Set ContractValidationOffline to true.
  3. Connect an EV and have it present an invalid or expired contract
     certificate.
  4. The Charging Station attempts local certificate validation and determines it
     is invalid.

Expected Result:
  - Local certificate validation fails.
  - The Charging Station does not proceed to look up the eMAID.
  - Charging is denied.

TEST CASE 14
------------
Title: Offline validation — LocalAuthorizeOffline is false

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is offline, ContractValidationOffline is true, and the
  contract certificate is locally valid, but LocalAuthorizeOffline is false,
  meaning the Charging Station should not fall back to the Local Authorization
  List or Cache.

Steps:
  1. Disconnect the Charging Station from the CSMS.
  2. Set ContractValidationOffline to true.
  3. Set LocalAuthorizeOffline to false.
  4. Connect an EV and have it present a valid contract certificate.
  5. The Charging Station validates the certificate locally (valid).
  6. Observe whether the Charging Station consults the Local Authorization List
     or Authorization Cache.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station does not look up the eMAID in the Local Authorization
    List or Authorization Cache.
  - Charging is denied since central authorization cannot be reached and local
    fallback is disabled.

TEST CASE 15
------------
Title: Charging Station comes back online after an offline period — resumes
       normal AuthorizeRequest flow

Scenario:
  The Charging Station was offline and is now back online. When the next EV
  connects, authorization should follow the standard online flow (send
  AuthorizeRequest to CSMS).

Steps:
  1. Start with the Charging Station in an offline state.
  2. Restore the connection between the Charging Station and the CSMS.
  3. Connect an EV and have it present a valid contract certificate.
  4. Observe the Charging Station behavior.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station detects it is back online.
  - The Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS (not attempting
    local offline validation).
  - The CSMS validates via OCSP and responds with an AuthorizeResponse.
  - Charging starts if the response is "Accepted".

TEST CASE 16
------------
Title: CSMS uses cached OCSP data when real-time OCSP is unavailable

Scenario:
  The CSMS cannot reach the OCSP server in real time but has a valid cached OCSP
  response for the presented certificate. The CSMS should use the cached data
  to validate (requirement C07.FR.05 permits real-time or cached OCSP data).

Steps:
  1. The Charging Station is online and the EV connects with a valid contract
     certificate.
  2. Simulate the OCSP server being unreachable from the CSMS.
  3. Ensure the CSMS has a valid, non-expired cached OCSP response for the
     contract certificate.
  4. The Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS.
  5. The CSMS attempts real-time OCSP but falls back to the cached OCSP
     response.
  6. The CSMS sends an AuthorizeResponse based on the cached data.

Expected Result:
  - The CSMS successfully uses cached OCSP data.
  - An AuthorizeResponse is returned with an appropriate status.
  - Charging proceeds normally if the cached OCSP response shows the certificate
    is good.

TEST CASE 17
------------
Title: CSMS receives AuthorizeRequest without OCSP hash data (malformed request)

Scenario:
  A Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest missing the
  iso15118CertificateHashData field, which is required by C07.FR.02. The CSMS
  must still respond with an AuthorizeResponse (C07.FR.04) and provide a
  meaningful rejection status.

Steps:
  1. Simulate or configure a Charging Station to send an AuthorizeRequest that
     contains only the eMAID but omits the iso15118CertificateHashData.
  2. The malformed request is received by the CSMS.
  3. Observe the CSMS response.

Expected Result:
  - The CSMS responds with an AuthorizeResponse.
  - The status indicates rejection (e.g., "Invalid" or an error reason).
  - The CSMS does not crash or leave the request unanswered.
  - Charging is not started.
