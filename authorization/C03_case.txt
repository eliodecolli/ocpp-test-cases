C03 - Authorization using Credit/Debit Card
Test Cases
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Protocol C03 describes how an EV Driver authorizes and starts a charging session
by using a credit or debit card at a payment terminal (either built into the
Charging Station or at a central kiosk). Authorization is handled externally by
a Payment System, which notifies the CSMS. The CSMS then generates a unique
IdToken of type Central and sends a RequestStartTransactionRequest to the
Charging Station to begin charging.

Actors: EV Driver, Payment System, CSMS, Charging Station

================================================================================
HAPPY PATH TEST CASES
================================================================================

TC-C03-01: Successful end-to-end authorization and charging start
-----------
Scenario:
  An EV Driver plugs in their vehicle and uses a valid credit card at the
  terminal. The payment is approved, and charging starts successfully.

Steps:
  1. The EV Driver plugs the charging cable into the vehicle and the Charging
     Station.
  2. Verify the Charging Station sends a StatusNotificationRequest to the CSMS
     reflecting the cable-connected state.
  3. Verify the Charging Station sends a TransactionEventRequest with
     eventType = Started to the CSMS.
  4. The EV Driver presents a valid credit card at the payment terminal.
  5. The terminal communicates with the payment back-office and the payment is
     approved.
  6. The Payment System sends an authorization message to the CSMS.
  7. Verify the CSMS generates a unique IdToken (IdTokenType = Central).
  8. Verify the CSMS sends a RequestStartTransactionRequest containing the
     generated IdToken to the Charging Station.
  9. Verify the Charging Station responds with a RequestStartTransactionResponse
     with status = Accepted.
  10. Verify the Charging Station begins delivering energy to the vehicle.
  11. Verify the Charging Station sends a TransactionEventRequest with
      eventType = Updated to the CSMS indicating charging has started.

Expected Result:
  The transaction is started and energy delivery begins. The CSMS receives
  confirmation that charging is active. All messages are exchanged in the
  correct sequence without errors.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C03-02: Successful authorization using a debit card
-----------
Scenario:
  Same as TC-C03-01 but the EV Driver uses a debit card instead of a credit
  card. The protocol behavior should be identical.

Steps:
  1. The EV Driver plugs in the charging cable.
  2. The EV Driver presents a valid debit card at the payment terminal.
  3. The terminal communicates with the payment back-office and approves the
     payment.
  4. The Payment System notifies the CSMS of authorization.
  5. Verify the CSMS generates a unique Central IdToken and sends a
     RequestStartTransactionRequest to the Charging Station.
  6. Verify the Charging Station responds with Accepted and starts charging.
  7. Verify the Charging Station sends a TransactionEventRequest with
     eventType = Updated.

Expected Result:
  The charging session starts successfully. No difference in behavior compared
  to a credit card transaction.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C03-03: Authorization via central payment kiosk serving multiple Charging Stations
-----------
Scenario:
  Multiple Charging Stations share one central payment terminal/kiosk. The EV
  Driver uses the kiosk to pay and selects the correct Charging Station.

Steps:
  1. The EV Driver plugs the cable into one of the group's Charging Stations.
  2. Verify that Charging Station sends StatusNotificationRequest and
     TransactionEventRequest (eventType = Started) to the CSMS.
  3. The EV Driver goes to the central kiosk, selects the correct Charging
     Station, and pays with a valid card.
  4. The kiosk's payment back-office approves the payment and notifies the CSMS,
     identifying which Charging Station the authorization is for.
  5. Verify the CSMS sends RequestStartTransactionRequest with a Central IdToken
     to the correct Charging Station only.
  6. Verify that Charging Station responds with Accepted and begins charging.
  7. Verify no RequestStartTransactionRequest is sent to the other Charging
     Stations in the group.

Expected Result:
  Only the selected Charging Station starts a session. Other stations in the
  group are unaffected.

================================================================================
REQUIREMENT-SPECIFIC TEST CASES
================================================================================

TC-C03-04: Charging Station does not send AuthorizeRequest for Central IdTokenType (C03.FR.01)
-----------
Scenario:
  When the CSMS sends a RequestStartTransactionRequest with a Central IdTokenType,
  the Charging Station must not attempt to independently re-authorize the token
  by sending an AuthorizeRequest back to the CSMS.

Steps:
  1. Complete the payment and authorization steps so that the CSMS sends a
     RequestStartTransactionRequest to the Charging Station with an IdToken of
     type Central.
  2. Monitor all messages sent from the Charging Station to the CSMS after
     receiving the RequestStartTransactionRequest.
  3. Verify the Charging Station does NOT send an AuthorizeRequest for the
     received Central IdToken.
  4. Verify the Charging Station sends a RequestStartTransactionResponse with
     status = Accepted.
  5. Verify the Charging Station starts charging normally.

Expected Result:
  No AuthorizeRequest is sent by the Charging Station. The Charging Station
  trusts the CSMS-initiated request directly and proceeds to accept and start
  the transaction.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C03-05: Central IdToken is not stored in the Authorization Cache (C03.FR.02)
-----------
Scenario:
  A Charging Station that has an Authorization Cache enabled must not cache any
  IdTokenInfo received for an IdToken of type Central, regardless of what
  message it arrived in.

Steps:
  1. Confirm the Charging Station has an Authorization Cache implemented and
     enabled.
  2. Complete the full C03 authorization flow so that the Charging Station
     receives an IdToken of type Central (e.g., via RequestStartTransactionRequest
     or any associated response).
  3. After the transaction completes, inspect the Charging Station's
     Authorization Cache (via diagnostics, configuration read, or a subsequent
     test probe).
  4. Verify that no entry for the Central IdToken exists in the cache.

Expected Result:
  The Authorization Cache contains no record of the Central IdToken or its
  associated IdTokenInfo. The cache is unchanged compared to before the
  transaction.

================================================================================
EDGE CASE TEST CASES
================================================================================

TC-C03-06: Cable plugged in but card payment is never completed
-----------
Scenario:
  The EV Driver plugs in the cable but walks away without paying. No
  authorization message is ever sent from the Payment System to the CSMS.

Steps:
  1. The EV Driver plugs the charging cable into the Charging Station.
  2. Verify the Charging Station sends StatusNotificationRequest and
     TransactionEventRequest (eventType = Started) to the CSMS.
  3. No payment card is presented. Wait for a reasonable timeout period.
  4. Verify the CSMS does not send a RequestStartTransactionRequest.
  5. Verify the Charging Station does not begin charging.
  6. Observe any timeout or idle state behavior on the Charging Station.

Expected Result:
  No charging session is started. The Charging Station remains in a waiting or
  idle state. No energy is delivered.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C03-07: Payment System sends authorization before cable is plugged in
-----------
Scenario:
  The Payment System authorizes a user before the EV Driver has plugged in the
  cable. The CSMS may receive an authorization without an active transaction
  event.

Steps:
  1. No cable is plugged in at the Charging Station.
  2. The EV Driver pays at the terminal and the Payment System sends an
     authorization message to the CSMS.
  3. The CSMS generates a Central IdToken and sends a
     RequestStartTransactionRequest to the Charging Station.
  4. Observe the Charging Station's response when no cable is connected.
  5. The EV Driver then plugs in the cable within the allowed window (if any).

Expected Result:
  The Charging Station either rejects the RequestStartTransactionRequest
  (because no cable is present) or holds the authorization pending cable
  connection, depending on implementation. Charging must not start without a
  connected cable.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C03-08: CSMS generates a non-unique IdToken (duplicate)
-----------
Scenario:
  The CSMS fails to generate a truly unique IdToken and produces a value that
  collides with an IdToken already in use by another active session.

Steps:
  1. Have at least one active charging session with a known Central IdToken.
  2. A new EV Driver completes payment and the Payment System notifies the CSMS.
  3. The CSMS (in this error scenario) generates an IdToken value that duplicates
     the one already in use.
  4. The CSMS sends a RequestStartTransactionRequest with the duplicate IdToken
     to the Charging Station.
  5. Observe the behavior of the Charging Station and CSMS.

Expected Result:
  Ideally the CSMS prevents duplicate IdToken generation. If a duplicate is
  sent, the Charging Station should still accept the request (as it does not
  validate uniqueness), but the CSMS must detect and handle the collision to
  prevent billing or session tracking errors. No existing session should be
  disrupted.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C03-09: Multiple rapid card taps on the same terminal
-----------
Scenario:
  An EV Driver taps their card multiple times rapidly (e.g., due to impatience).
  The Payment System might send multiple authorization messages to the CSMS.

Steps:
  1. The EV Driver plugs in the cable.
  2. The EV Driver taps the same card multiple times at the terminal in quick
     succession.
  3. The Payment System sends one or more authorization messages to the CSMS.
  4. The CSMS processes the message(s) and sends RequestStartTransactionRequest
     to the Charging Station.
  5. Observe whether multiple requests are sent to the Charging Station and how
     it handles them.

Expected Result:
  Only one charging session is started. Duplicate authorization messages should
  be deduplicated by the Payment System or CSMS. The Charging Station must not
  start multiple overlapping sessions for the same connector.

================================================================================
FAILURE / NEGATIVE TEST CASES
================================================================================

TC-C03-10: Payment is declined by the Payment System
-----------
Scenario:
  The EV Driver presents a card that is declined (e.g., insufficient funds,
  expired card). The Payment System does not send an authorization to the CSMS.

Steps:
  1. The EV Driver plugs in the charging cable.
  2. The EV Driver presents a card that will be declined.
  3. The payment terminal or back-office declines the transaction.
  4. Verify the Payment System does NOT send an authorization message to the
     CSMS.
  5. Verify the CSMS does NOT send a RequestStartTransactionRequest.
  6. Verify the Charging Station does NOT begin charging.
  7. Verify the EV Driver receives an appropriate decline indication at the
     terminal.

Expected Result:
  No charging session is initiated. The Charging Station remains idle. The
  decline is visible to the EV Driver at the terminal.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C03-11: CSMS is unreachable when Payment System sends authorization
-----------
Scenario:
  The CSMS is offline or unreachable at the moment the Payment System sends the
  authorization message. The CSMS cannot forward the start request to the
  Charging Station.

Steps:
  1. The EV Driver plugs in the cable.
  2. The EV Driver completes payment and the Payment System attempts to send
     authorization to the CSMS.
  3. The CSMS is unavailable (network outage or service down).
  4. Observe whether the Payment System retries or reports an error.
  5. Verify the Charging Station does not start charging during the outage.
  6. Once the CSMS comes back online, observe whether the authorization can be
     replayed or whether the session must be restarted.

Expected Result:
  Charging does not start while the CSMS is unreachable. The EV Driver is
  notified of the failure. When the CSMS recovers, it handles the pending
  authorization gracefully (either retries or requires a new payment).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C03-12: Charging Station rejects the RequestStartTransactionRequest
-----------
Scenario:
  The Charging Station receives a valid RequestStartTransactionRequest from the
  CSMS but responds with a status other than Accepted (e.g., Rejected or a
  fault condition).

Steps:
  1. The EV Driver completes payment and the CSMS sends a valid
     RequestStartTransactionRequest with a Central IdToken.
  2. The Charging Station is in a state where it cannot start a session (e.g.,
     a fault, connector unavailable, or firmware issue).
  3. The Charging Station responds with RequestStartTransactionResponse with
     status != Accepted.
  4. Observe the CSMS behavior after receiving the rejection.
  5. Verify no charging occurs.

Expected Result:
  The CSMS receives the rejection response and must not assume charging has
  started. The CSMS should inform the Payment System or EV Driver that the
  session could not be started (and a refund or void may be required). The
  Charging Station sends no TransactionEventRequest with eventType = Updated for
  active charging.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C03-13: Communication failure between CSMS and Charging Station after payment approval
-----------
Scenario:
  The Payment System successfully authorizes the transaction and notifies the
  CSMS, but the connection between the CSMS and the Charging Station is lost
  before the RequestStartTransactionRequest is delivered.

Steps:
  1. The EV Driver plugs in and pays. The Payment System notifies the CSMS.
  2. The CSMS generates a Central IdToken and attempts to send
     RequestStartTransactionRequest to the Charging Station.
  3. The network link between the CSMS and Charging Station drops before the
     message is received.
  4. Observe whether the CSMS retries the request once the connection is
     restored.
  5. Observe the Charging Station state during the outage.
  6. Verify the Charging Station does not start charging without receiving the
     valid RequestStartTransactionRequest.

Expected Result:
  The Charging Station does not start charging on its own. Once reconnected, the
  CSMS retries the request or handles the outstanding authorization. The EV
  Driver is informed of the delay or failure.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C03-14: RequestStartTransactionRequest received without a prior payment authorization
-----------
Scenario:
  A RequestStartTransactionRequest with a Central IdToken arrives at the Charging
  Station, but it was not preceded by a valid Payment System authorization (e.g.,
  a replayed or spoofed message).

Steps:
  1. No EV Driver has made a payment at the terminal.
  2. A RequestStartTransactionRequest with IdTokenType = Central is sent to the
     Charging Station (simulating a replay or out-of-order message).
  3. Observe whether the Charging Station processes and accepts the request.
  4. Verify whether the CSMS has any mechanism to detect that no corresponding
     payment authorization exists.

Expected Result:
  From the Charging Station's perspective, it accepts any valid
  RequestStartTransactionRequest from the CSMS regardless of payment history
  (payment validation is the CSMS's responsibility). The CSMS should have
  safeguards to only send such requests in response to legitimate Payment System
  authorizations. This test validates that the overall system has proper
  authorization controls at the CSMS level.
