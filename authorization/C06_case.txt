C06 - Authorization using Local Id Type - Test Cases
======================================================
Protocol: OCPP 2.0.1 | Functional Block: C. Authorization
Goal: Enable a Charging Station to start and stop charging using a locally generated
IdToken (e.g., a parking ticket), without requiring an RFID card.

======================================================
HAPPY PATH TEST CASES
======================================================

TC-C06-01: Successful authorization and charging session with parking ticket
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Full happy-path session - parking ticket start to payment stop

Scenario:
An EV driver enters a garage, takes a parking ticket, parks at a Charging Station,
uses the ticket to start charging, later pays at a kiosk, and the session is stopped
remotely by the payment terminal via the CSMS.

Steps:
1. The EV driver collects a parking ticket at the garage entrance barrier.
2. The driver parks the EV at a Charging Station and plugs in the charging cable.
3. The driver scans or inserts the parking ticket at the Charging Station.
4. The Charging Station generates a Local IdToken from the parking ticket data.
5. The Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS containing the Local IdToken.
6. The CSMS receives the AuthorizeRequest and evaluates the token.
7. The CSMS responds with an AuthorizeResponse containing status "Accepted".
8. The Charging Station begins supplying energy to the EV.
9. The driver leaves. The EV continues to charge.
10. The driver returns and inserts the parking ticket into the payment kiosk.
11. The driver pays for parking and charging.
12. The payment terminal sends a stop command to the CSMS.
13. The CSMS relays the stop command to the Charging Station.
14. The Charging Station stops the charging session.
15. The driver unplugs the cable and drives away.

Expected Result:
- The AuthorizeRequest is sent with IdTokenType = Local.
- The CSMS returns AuthorizeResponse with status = Accepted.
- Energy is delivered only after authorization is received.
- The charging session stops cleanly upon the remote stop command from the payment terminal.
- The driver can unplug and leave without error.

----------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C06-02: CSMS accepts authorization on first attempt
-------------------------------------------------------
Title: AuthorizeRequest accepted immediately with no delay

Scenario:
The CSMS is online and responds promptly to an AuthorizeRequest for a Local IdToken.

Steps:
1. Driver plugs in EV and presents a parking ticket at the Charging Station.
2. Charging Station sends AuthorizeRequest with IdTokenType = Local to the CSMS.
3. CSMS immediately responds with AuthorizeResponse status = Accepted.
4. Charging Station starts delivering energy.

Expected Result:
- No perceptible delay before charging begins.
- Energy is supplied only after the Accepted response is received.
- No error or warning messages are generated.

======================================================
EDGE CASE TEST CASES
======================================================

TC-C06-03: Charging Station does not offer energy before authorization
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Energy is withheld until AuthorizeResponse is received

Scenario:
Verifies that the Charging Station enforces the rule that energy must not be offered
prior to receiving authorization (C06.FR.01).

Steps:
1. Driver plugs in the EV and presents the parking ticket.
2. The Charging Station generates a Local IdToken and sends AuthorizeRequest.
3. Before the AuthorizeResponse arrives, observe the Charging Station behavior.
4. AuthorizeResponse arrives with status = Accepted.
5. Observe when energy delivery begins.

Expected Result:
- The Charging Station does NOT supply energy before the AuthorizeResponse is received.
- Energy delivery begins only after the Accepted status is confirmed.

----------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C06-04: AuthorizeRequest used only for charging authorization purposes
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: AuthorizeRequest is not used for non-charging purposes

Scenario:
Verifies that the AuthorizeRequest message is scoped only to authorizing identifiers
for charging and is not sent for unrelated purposes (C06.FR.03).

Steps:
1. Observe system behavior during typical Charging Station operation.
2. Note all instances where AuthorizeRequest messages are sent.
3. Verify the context and purpose of each AuthorizeRequest message.

Expected Result:
- AuthorizeRequest messages are sent only when authorizing an identifier to start
  or manage a charging session.
- No AuthorizeRequest messages are sent for unrelated administrative tasks.

----------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C06-05: Parking ticket scanned but EV not yet plugged in
------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Local IdToken presented before cable is connected

Scenario:
The driver scans the parking ticket at the Charging Station before plugging in the
charging cable.

Steps:
1. Driver scans or inserts the parking ticket at the Charging Station.
2. The Charging Station generates a Local IdToken and sends AuthorizeRequest.
3. CSMS responds with AuthorizeResponse status = Accepted.
4. Driver then plugs in the charging cable.

Expected Result:
- The Charging Station handles the out-of-order sequence gracefully.
- Once the cable is plugged in and authorization is already accepted, charging begins.
- No error causes the session to be permanently blocked.

----------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C06-06: Same parking ticket used twice simultaneously at different connectors
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Duplicate Local IdToken presented on two connectors at the same time

Scenario:
Two drivers (unlikely but possible in a shared-ticket scenario or a system error)
attempt to use what appears to be the same Local IdToken on two connectors.

Steps:
1. Driver A presents parking ticket X at Connector 1.
2. Charging Station sends AuthorizeRequest for token X to CSMS.
3. Almost simultaneously, a second request for token X arrives (or Driver B presents
   the same token at Connector 2).
4. CSMS processes both AuthorizeRequests.

Expected Result:
- The CSMS evaluates each AuthorizeRequest independently.
- The CSMS responds with an appropriate AuthorizeResponse to each request.
- The system does not crash or enter an inconsistent state.
- Policy-level decisions (e.g., rejecting the second use of a token) are reflected in
  the authorization status, not in protocol errors.

----------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C06-07: Driver does not return to pay - session left running
---------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Charging session continues indefinitely when no stop command is received

Scenario:
The driver never returns to pay, so the payment terminal never sends a stop command.

Steps:
1. Driver completes the normal authorization and charging session start.
2. Driver does not return and does not pay.
3. No stop command is ever sent by the payment terminal or CSMS.

Expected Result:
- The Charging Station continues charging until another mechanism intervenes
  (e.g., a maximum session duration limit, cable disconnection, or operator action).
- The system does not enter an error state solely because no stop command arrives.
- The session remains in a valid, trackable state in the CSMS.

======================================================
FAILURE / REJECTION SCENARIO TEST CASES
======================================================

TC-C06-08: CSMS rejects the Local IdToken
------------------------------------------
Title: AuthorizeResponse returned with status other than Accepted

Scenario:
The CSMS determines that the presented Local IdToken is not valid (e.g., the parking
ticket is expired, unknown, or already used) and rejects the authorization request.

Steps:
1. Driver plugs in the EV and presents the parking ticket.
2. Charging Station sends AuthorizeRequest with IdTokenType = Local to the CSMS.
3. CSMS responds with AuthorizeResponse and an authorization status indicating
   rejection (e.g., Invalid, Expired, or Unknown).

Expected Result:
- The Charging Station receives the rejection status.
- The Charging Station does NOT supply energy to the EV.
- The driver is notified (e.g., via display or indicator) that authorization failed.
- No charging session is started.

----------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C06-09: CSMS is unreachable when AuthorizeRequest is sent
-------------------------------------------------------------
Title: No response from CSMS after AuthorizeRequest is transmitted

Scenario:
The network connection between the Charging Station and the CSMS is lost just after
the driver presents the parking ticket, so the CSMS never receives or responds to
the AuthorizeRequest.

Steps:
1. Driver plugs in the EV and presents the parking ticket.
2. Charging Station attempts to send AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS.
3. The CSMS is unreachable; no AuthorizeResponse is received.

Expected Result:
- The Charging Station does NOT supply energy (C06.FR.01 - energy only after
  authorization).
- The Charging Station handles the timeout gracefully without crashing.
- The driver is informed that authorization could not be completed.
- The Charging Station may retry or wait according to its configured behavior,
  but must not start charging without an Accepted authorization status.

----------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C06-10: CSMS receives AuthorizeRequest but does not include a status in the response
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: AuthorizeResponse is malformed or missing the authorization status field

Scenario:
The CSMS responds to the AuthorizeRequest but the response is missing the required
authorization status value (violating C06.FR.04).

Steps:
1. Driver presents parking ticket; Charging Station sends AuthorizeRequest.
2. CSMS sends back a malformed AuthorizeResponse that does not include a valid
   authorization status value.
3. Charging Station attempts to parse the response.

Expected Result:
- The Charging Station treats the missing or invalid status as a failed authorization.
- No energy is supplied to the EV.
- An error or warning is logged at the Charging Station and/or CSMS.

----------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C06-11: Payment terminal sends stop command before driver returns
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Remote stop issued while EV is still charging

Scenario:
The payment kiosk or an operator sends a stop command to the CSMS before the driver
has physically returned to the Charging Station.

Steps:
1. Normal authorization and charging session are active.
2. Payment terminal (or operator) sends a stop command to the CSMS.
3. CSMS relays the stop command to the Charging Station.
4. Charging Station stops energy delivery.
5. Driver returns later to unplug the cable.

Expected Result:
- Energy delivery stops promptly upon receipt of the remote stop command.
- The session is recorded as completed in the CSMS.
- The driver can safely unplug the cable when they return.
- No energy is supplied after the session has been stopped.

----------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C06-12: Driver attempts to unplug cable while session is still active
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Cable disconnected by driver before payment and remote stop are completed

Scenario:
The driver unplugs the charging cable without first paying or waiting for the remote
stop command.

Steps:
1. Normal authorization and charging session are active.
2. Driver physically disconnects the charging cable from the EV or the Charging Station.
3. Charging Station detects the cable disconnect event.

Expected Result:
- The Charging Station halts energy delivery immediately upon detecting the
  cable disconnect.
- The CSMS is notified of the unexpected session end.
- The session is recorded with appropriate status (e.g., disconnected without proper
  stop) so that billing or reconciliation can occur.

----------------------------------------------------------------------

TC-C06-13: Non-Local IdToken type presented (RFID card used instead of parking ticket)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Authorization attempted with RFID (non-Local IdTokenType) - out of scope for C06

Scenario:
Verifies that presenting an RFID card does not trigger the Local IdToken authorization
flow described in C06. This case is handled by a different use case, not C06.

Steps:
1. Driver presents an RFID card at the Charging Station instead of a parking ticket.
2. Charging Station reads the IdToken with type ISO14443 (or similar RFID type),
   not Local.
3. Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest with the appropriate RFID IdTokenType.

Expected Result:
- The Charging Station processes this as a non-Local authorization flow.
- The C06-specific Local IdToken flow is NOT triggered.
- Authorization proceeds via the correct RFID use case.
- No error occurs as a result of using an RFID card.
