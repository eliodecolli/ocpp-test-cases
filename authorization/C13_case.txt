Test Cases for C13 - Offline Authorization through Local Authorization List
OCPP 2.0.1 Edition 2
=========================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Use case C13 describes how a Charging Station authorizes an EV Driver's IdToken
when it cannot communicate with the Charge Station Management System (CSMS), i.e.
when it is offline. Authorization is performed by checking a Local Authorization
List (LAL) stored on the Charging Station itself.

=========================================================================
HAPPY PATH TEST CASES
=========================================================================

TC-C13-01: Successful offline authorization with Accepted token
---------------------------------------------------------------
Title: EV Driver authorized offline via Local Authorization List

Scenario:
  The Charging Station loses connection to the CSMS. An EV Driver presents an
  IdToken that is present in the Local Authorization List with status "Accepted".

Prerequisites:
  - Local Authorization List is loaded on the Charging Station.
  - LocalAuthListEnabled is set to true.
  - The Charging Station is offline (connection to CSMS is lost).
  - The EV Driver's IdToken exists in the Local Authorization List with status "Accepted".
  - OfflineTxForUnknownIdEnabled is false.

Steps:
  1. Simulate or confirm the Charging Station has lost its connection to the CSMS.
  2. The EV Driver presents their IdToken (e.g., RFID card) at the Charging Station.
  3. The Charging Station checks its Local Authorization List for the presented IdToken.
  4. Observe the Charging Station's response.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station finds the IdToken in the Local Authorization List with status "Accepted".
  - The Charging Station accepts the IdToken and authorizes the session locally without contacting the CSMS.
  - The connector is locked and energy offer is started.
  - Charging begins successfully.


TC-C13-02: Optional tariff notification displayed during offline authorization
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Cached tariff information shown to EV Driver during offline session

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is offline and authorizes a token from the Local Authorization
  List. A cached tariff (e.g., 0.23/kWh) is available and displayed to the EV Driver.

Prerequisites:
  - All prerequisites from TC-C13-01 apply.
  - A cached tariff (e.g., 0.23/kWh) is stored on the Charging Station.

Steps:
  1. Simulate or confirm the Charging Station has lost its connection to the CSMS.
  2. The EV Driver presents an IdToken that is "Accepted" in the Local Authorization List.
  3. The Charging Station checks its Local Authorization List and finds an "Accepted" entry.
  4. Observe whether the tariff notification is provided to the EV Driver.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station optionally notifies the EV Driver of the cached tariff (e.g., 0.23/kWh).
  - The connector is locked and charging starts.
  - The optional notification does not block or delay authorization.


=========================================================================
EDGE CASE TEST CASES
=========================================================================

TC-C13-03: Local Authorization List entry takes priority over Authorization Cache
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: LAL entry overrides conflicting Authorization Cache entry for the same IdToken

Scenario:
  Both the Local Authorization List (LAL) and the Authorization Cache are supported
  and contain entries for the same IdToken but with different statuses. The LAL entry
  must take priority.

Prerequisites:
  - The Charging Station supports both the Authorization Cache and the Local Authorization List.
  - LocalAuthListEnabled is set to true.
  - The Charging Station is offline.
  - The EV Driver's IdToken is present in both the Authorization Cache and the Local Authorization List.
  - The Authorization Cache has status "Blocked" for the IdToken.
  - The Local Authorization List has status "Accepted" for the IdToken.

Steps:
  1. Simulate or confirm the Charging Station is offline.
  2. The EV Driver presents the IdToken that exists in both stores.
  3. The Charging Station evaluates both the Local Authorization List and the Authorization Cache.
  4. Observe which result is applied.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station uses the Local Authorization List entry, ignoring the conflicting Authorization Cache entry.
  - The IdToken is accepted because the LAL status is "Accepted".
  - Charging begins successfully.
  - The Authorization Cache entry does not override the LAL decision.


TC-C13-04: Local Authorization List entry takes priority — Cache says Accepted, LAL says Blocked
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: LAL "Blocked" entry overrides Authorization Cache "Accepted" entry

Scenario:
  The Authorization Cache has status "Accepted" for an IdToken, but the Local
  Authorization List has status "Blocked". The LAL must take priority.

Prerequisites:
  - The Charging Station supports both the Authorization Cache and the Local Authorization List.
  - LocalAuthListEnabled is set to true.
  - The Charging Station is offline.
  - The EV Driver's IdToken is in the Authorization Cache with status "Accepted".
  - The same IdToken is in the Local Authorization List with status "Blocked".

Steps:
  1. Simulate or confirm the Charging Station is offline.
  2. The EV Driver presents the IdToken.
  3. The Charging Station evaluates both the Local Authorization List and the Authorization Cache.
  4. Observe the authorization decision.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station uses the Local Authorization List entry.
  - The IdToken is rejected because the LAL status is "Blocked".
  - Charging does not start.
  - The Authorization Cache "Accepted" entry is not used.


TC-C13-05: IdToken in LAL with status other than Accepted (e.g., Blocked or Expired)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: IdToken present in LAL but not with "Accepted" status is rejected offline

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is offline. An EV Driver presents an IdToken that exists
  in the Local Authorization List but with a non-Accepted status such as "Blocked",
  "Expired", or "Invalid".

Prerequisites:
  - LocalAuthListEnabled is set to true.
  - The Charging Station is offline.
  - The EV Driver's IdToken is in the Local Authorization List with a status other than "Accepted" (e.g., "Blocked").
  - OfflineTxForUnknownIdEnabled is false.

Steps:
  1. Simulate or confirm the Charging Station is offline.
  2. The EV Driver presents the IdToken.
  3. The Charging Station checks the Local Authorization List and finds the IdToken with a non-Accepted status.
  4. Observe the authorization response.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station rejects the IdToken.
  - Charging does not start.
  - The EV Driver is notified that their token is not authorized.


TC-C13-06: Offline authorization when LocalAuthListEnabled is false
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Local Authorization List is disabled — offline authorization via LAL fails

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is offline but the Local Authorization List feature has
  been disabled via configuration. Authorization via the LAL should not occur.

Prerequisites:
  - LocalAuthListEnabled is set to false.
  - The Charging Station is offline.
  - The EV Driver's IdToken is in the Local Authorization List with status "Accepted".
  - OfflineTxForUnknownIdEnabled is false.

Steps:
  1. Set LocalAuthListEnabled to false on the Charging Station.
  2. Simulate or confirm the Charging Station is offline.
  3. The EV Driver presents the IdToken.
  4. The Charging Station attempts to authorize the IdToken.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station does not use the Local Authorization List.
  - The IdToken is not authorized offline.
  - Charging does not start.


TC-C13-07: OfflineTxForUnknownIdEnabled is true — unknown token is accepted offline
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Any IdToken accepted offline when OfflineTxForUnknownIdEnabled is true

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is offline and OfflineTxForUnknownIdEnabled is set to true.
  An EV Driver presents an IdToken that is NOT present in the Local Authorization List
  or Authorization Cache. The token should still be accepted.

Prerequisites:
  - LocalAuthListEnabled is set to true (or false — does not affect this requirement).
  - The Charging Station is offline.
  - OfflineTxForUnknownIdEnabled is set to true.
  - The EV Driver's IdToken is NOT present in the Local Authorization List or Authorization Cache.

Steps:
  1. Set OfflineTxForUnknownIdEnabled to true on the Charging Station.
  2. Simulate or confirm the Charging Station is offline.
  3. The EV Driver presents an IdToken that is not in the Local Authorization List.
  4. The Charging Station attempts to authorize the IdToken.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station allows the unknown IdToken to authorize a transaction.
  - Charging begins.
  - The unknown identifier is not rejected.


TC-C13-08: Offline authorization attempted with an empty Local Authorization List
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: No entries in Local Authorization List — known token cannot be authorized offline

Scenario:
  The Local Authorization List has not been populated (it is empty). The Charging
  Station is offline and an EV Driver presents their IdToken.

Prerequisites:
  - LocalAuthListEnabled is set to true.
  - The Local Authorization List is empty (no entries).
  - The Charging Station is offline.
  - OfflineTxForUnknownIdEnabled is false.

Steps:
  1. Confirm the Local Authorization List is empty on the Charging Station.
  2. Simulate or confirm the Charging Station is offline.
  3. The EV Driver presents their IdToken.
  4. The Charging Station checks the Local Authorization List.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station finds no entry for the IdToken in the Local Authorization List.
  - The IdToken is rejected.
  - Charging does not start.


=========================================================================
FAILURE / NEGATIVE TEST CASES
=========================================================================

TC-C13-09: IdToken not found in Local Authorization List while offline
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Unknown IdToken rejected offline when OfflineTxForUnknownIdEnabled is false

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is offline. An EV Driver presents an IdToken that is not
  present in the Local Authorization List. OfflineTxForUnknownIdEnabled is false,
  so unknown identifiers must not be authorized.

Prerequisites:
  - LocalAuthListEnabled is set to true.
  - The Charging Station is offline.
  - The EV Driver's IdToken is NOT in the Local Authorization List.
  - OfflineTxForUnknownIdEnabled is false.

Steps:
  1. Simulate or confirm the Charging Station is offline.
  2. The EV Driver presents an IdToken not found in the Local Authorization List.
  3. The Charging Station searches the Local Authorization List and finds no matching entry.
  4. Observe the authorization result.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station rejects the IdToken.
  - Charging does not start.
  - The EV Driver is informed their token was not accepted.


TC-C13-10: Charging Station comes back online — verify normal authorization resumes
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Reconnection to CSMS restores online authorization flow

Scenario:
  After a period of offline operation (using the Local Authorization List), the
  Charging Station reconnects to the CSMS. Subsequent authorization requests should
  be handled by the CSMS again, not solely by the LAL.

Prerequisites:
  - The Charging Station was previously offline and authorizing via the LAL.
  - The Charging Station re-establishes connection to the CSMS.

Steps:
  1. Confirm the Charging Station has regained its connection to the CSMS.
  2. The EV Driver presents an IdToken.
  3. The Charging Station processes the authorization request.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station sends the authorization request to the CSMS (online path).
  - The session follows the standard online authorization use case, not C13.
  - The Local Authorization List may still be used as a fallback if configured, but the CSMS is the primary authority.


TC-C13-11: Local Authorization List not available on the Charging Station
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Missing Local Authorization List prevents offline authorization

Scenario:
  The Charging Station is offline, but no Local Authorization List has ever been
  downloaded or is unavailable. Offline authorization via the LAL cannot proceed.

Prerequisites:
  - Local Authorization List is not available (never synchronized or cleared).
  - The Charging Station is offline.
  - OfflineTxForUnknownIdEnabled is false.

Steps:
  1. Confirm the Local Authorization List is absent or unavailable on the Charging Station.
  2. Simulate or confirm the Charging Station is offline.
  3. The EV Driver presents their IdToken.
  4. The Charging Station attempts to perform offline authorization.

Expected Result:
  - The Charging Station cannot authorize the IdToken offline.
  - The IdToken is rejected.
  - Charging does not start.
  - A failure postcondition is reached: the Charging Station does not accept tokens when the LAL is unavailable.
