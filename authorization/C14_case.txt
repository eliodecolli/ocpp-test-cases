C14 - Online Authorization through Local Authorization List
Test Cases
============================================================

Protocol Summary:
Use case C14 describes how a Charging Station authorizes an EV Driver's IdToken using its
Local Authorization List while the Charging Station is online. If the IdToken is present
in the list with status "Accepted," the Charging Station may start charging immediately
without contacting the CSMS. If the IdToken is absent or has a non-Accepted status, the
Charging Station must fall back to sending an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS.

============================================================
TEST CASE 1
Title: Happy Path - IdToken found in Local Authorization List with Accepted status

Scenario:
The EV Driver presents an IdToken that exists in the Local Authorization List with a
status of Accepted. The Charging Station is online, the Local Authorization List is
enabled, and LocalPreAuthorize is set to true.

Steps:
1. Confirm the Local Authorization List is available and enabled (LocalAuthListEnabled = true).
2. Confirm LocalPreAuthorize is set to true.
3. Add an IdToken to the Local Authorization List with status "Accepted."
4. Bring the Charging Station online.
5. Have the EV Driver present the IdToken at the Charging Station.
6. Observe whether the Charging Station checks the Local Authorization List.
7. Observe whether the Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS.
8. Observe whether the Charging Station locks the connector and starts the energy offer.

Expected Result:
The Charging Station finds the IdToken in the Local Authorization List with status
Accepted, does NOT send an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS, locks the connector, and
starts charging immediately. (Satisfies requirement C14.FR.02)

------------------------------------------------------------
TEST CASE 2
Title: Happy Path - IdToken not in Local Authorization List; CSMS grants authorization

Scenario:
The EV Driver presents an IdToken that is not present in the Local Authorization List.
The Charging Station is online and must fall back to requesting authorization from the
CSMS, which responds with Accepted.

Steps:
1. Confirm the Local Authorization List is available and enabled.
2. Ensure the presented IdToken is NOT present in the Local Authorization List.
3. Bring the Charging Station online.
4. Have the EV Driver present the IdToken at the Charging Station.
5. Observe that the Charging Station checks the Local Authorization List and does not
   find the IdToken.
6. Observe that the Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS.
7. Configure the CSMS to respond with AuthorizeResponse(Accepted).
8. Observe whether the Charging Station locks the connector and starts the energy offer.

Expected Result:
The Charging Station does not find the IdToken locally, sends an AuthorizeRequest to
the CSMS, receives an Accepted response, locks the connector, and starts charging.
(Satisfies requirement C14.FR.03)

------------------------------------------------------------
TEST CASE 3
Title: IdToken in Local Authorization List with non-Accepted status; CSMS grants authorization

Scenario:
The EV Driver presents an IdToken that exists in the Local Authorization List but with
a status other than Accepted (e.g., Blocked, Expired, or Invalid). The Charging Station
must fall back to sending an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS.

Steps:
1. Confirm the Local Authorization List is available and enabled.
2. Add an IdToken to the Local Authorization List with a status of "Blocked" (or any
   status other than Accepted).
3. Bring the Charging Station online.
4. Have the EV Driver present that IdToken at the Charging Station.
5. Observe that the Charging Station checks the Local Authorization List and finds the
   IdToken with a non-Accepted status.
6. Observe that the Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS.
7. Configure the CSMS to respond with AuthorizeResponse(Accepted).
8. Observe whether the Charging Station locks the connector and starts charging.

Expected Result:
The Charging Station finds the IdToken in the Local Authorization List with a
non-Accepted status, sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS, receives an Accepted
response, and starts charging. (Satisfies requirement C14.FR.03)

------------------------------------------------------------
TEST CASE 4
Title: IdToken in Local Authorization List with non-Accepted status; CSMS denies authorization

Scenario:
The EV Driver presents an IdToken that is in the Local Authorization List with a
non-Accepted status, and the CSMS also denies authorization.

Steps:
1. Confirm the Local Authorization List is available and enabled.
2. Add an IdToken to the Local Authorization List with a status of "Expired."
3. Bring the Charging Station online.
4. Have the EV Driver present that IdToken at the Charging Station.
5. Observe that the Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS.
6. Configure the CSMS to respond with a non-Accepted status (e.g., Invalid or Blocked).
7. Observe whether the Charging Station starts charging or rejects the session.

Expected Result:
The Charging Station receives a non-Accepted response from the CSMS, does NOT lock the
connector, and does NOT start charging. The authorization is rejected.

------------------------------------------------------------
TEST CASE 5
Title: Local Authorization List is disabled; Charging Station must contact CSMS

Scenario:
The Local Authorization List feature is disabled via the LocalAuthListEnabled
configuration variable. Even if IdTokens would normally be in the list, the Charging
Station must not use it and must contact the CSMS for all authorizations.

Steps:
1. Set LocalAuthListEnabled to false on the Charging Station.
2. Ensure an IdToken is present in the Local Authorization List data structure with
   status Accepted.
3. Bring the Charging Station online.
4. Have the EV Driver present that IdToken at the Charging Station.
5. Observe whether the Charging Station consults the Local Authorization List.
6. Observe whether the Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS.

Expected Result:
Because the Local Authorization List is disabled, the Charging Station does not use it
and sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS for every IdToken, regardless of what is
stored in the list.

------------------------------------------------------------
TEST CASE 6
Title: Local Authorization List has priority over Authorization Cache for the same IdToken

Scenario:
Both the Local Authorization List and the Authorization Cache are supported and enabled.
The same IdToken exists in both, but with different statuses (e.g., Blocked in the
Authorization Cache and Accepted in the Local Authorization List).

Steps:
1. Confirm both the Authorization Cache and Local Authorization List are available and
   enabled.
2. Add the IdToken to the Authorization Cache with status "Blocked."
3. Add the same IdToken to the Local Authorization List with status "Accepted."
4. Bring the Charging Station online.
5. Have the EV Driver present that IdToken at the Charging Station.
6. Observe which data source the Charging Station uses to make the authorization decision.
7. Observe whether an AuthorizeRequest is sent to the CSMS.
8. Observe whether the Charging Station starts charging.

Expected Result:
The Charging Station uses the Local Authorization List entry (Accepted) and gives it
priority over the Authorization Cache entry (Blocked). The Charging Station starts
charging without sending an AuthorizeRequest. (Satisfies requirement C14.FR.01)

------------------------------------------------------------
TEST CASE 7
Title: Authorization Cache has priority is NOT given over Local Authorization List

Scenario:
This is the inverse of Test Case 6. The IdToken has status Accepted in the
Authorization Cache but is Blocked in the Local Authorization List. Confirms that the
Local Authorization List always wins.

Steps:
1. Confirm both the Authorization Cache and Local Authorization List are available and
   enabled.
2. Add the IdToken to the Authorization Cache with status "Accepted."
3. Add the same IdToken to the Local Authorization List with status "Blocked."
4. Bring the Charging Station online.
5. Have the EV Driver present that IdToken at the Charging Station.
6. Observe which data source the Charging Station uses to make the authorization decision.

Expected Result:
The Charging Station uses the Local Authorization List entry (Blocked), not the
Authorization Cache entry. It sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS because the local
status is not Accepted, rather than immediately authorizing from the cache.
(Satisfies requirement C14.FR.01)

------------------------------------------------------------
TEST CASE 8
Title: Empty Local Authorization List - Charging Station falls back to CSMS

Scenario:
The Local Authorization List is enabled but contains no entries. Any presented IdToken
will not be found and must be authorized via the CSMS.

Steps:
1. Confirm the Local Authorization List is enabled and available but contains zero entries
   (LocalAuthListEntries = 0).
2. Bring the Charging Station online.
3. Have the EV Driver present an IdToken.
4. Observe that the Charging Station searches the Local Authorization List and finds
   no match.
5. Observe that the Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS.
6. Configure the CSMS to respond with Accepted.
7. Observe whether charging starts.

Expected Result:
The Charging Station finds no match in the empty list, sends an AuthorizeRequest to the
CSMS, receives Accepted, and starts charging.

------------------------------------------------------------
TEST CASE 9
Title: Local Authorization List at maximum capacity - New entry accepted and usable

Scenario:
The Local Authorization List is at its maximum number of entries
(LocalAuthListEntries reports the maximum). A new update is sent and a new IdToken is
added. The new IdToken should still be authorizable.

Steps:
1. Fill the Local Authorization List to its reported maximum capacity.
2. Send a SendLocalList update to add one more IdToken with status Accepted (replacing
   or incrementally adding as supported).
3. Bring the Charging Station online.
4. Have the EV Driver present the newly added IdToken.
5. Observe whether the Charging Station finds it in the Local Authorization List and
   starts charging without contacting the CSMS.

Expected Result:
If the update was successfully applied, the Charging Station finds the new IdToken with
status Accepted and starts charging without sending an AuthorizeRequest.

------------------------------------------------------------
TEST CASE 10
Title: LocalPreAuthorize is false - Charging Station contacts CSMS even for Accepted entries

Scenario:
LocalPreAuthorize is set to false. Even though the IdToken is in the Local Authorization
List with status Accepted, the Charging Station should not locally authorize without
contacting the CSMS first (per prerequisites of the use case).

Steps:
1. Set LocalPreAuthorize to false.
2. Add an IdToken to the Local Authorization List with status Accepted.
3. Bring the Charging Station online.
4. Have the EV Driver present that IdToken.
5. Observe whether the Charging Station sends an AuthorizeRequest to the CSMS.

Expected Result:
Because LocalPreAuthorize is false, the Charging Station does not use the Local
Authorization List for immediate authorization and instead sends an AuthorizeRequest to
the CSMS. This tests that the prerequisite (LocalPreAuthorize = true) is enforced
before C14.FR.02 applies.

TEST CASE 11
Title: Tariff notification is provided when tariff is cached in the Local Authorization List

Scenario:
The Local Authorization List entry for the IdToken includes a cached tariff
(e.g., 0.23/kWh). After local authorization, the Charging Station optionally displays
or notifies the EV Driver of the tariff before starting the session.

Steps:
1. Add an IdToken to the Local Authorization List with status Accepted and an associated
   cached tariff of 0.23/kWh.
2. Bring the Charging Station online.
3. Have the EV Driver present the IdToken.
4. Observe whether the Charging Station locally authorizes without contacting the CSMS.
5. Observe whether a tariff notification is issued to the EV Driver (optional behavior
   as shown in the sequence diagram).
6. Observe whether the connector locks and charging begins.

Expected Result:
The Charging Station locally authorizes the IdToken, optionally notifies the EV Driver
of the tariff (0.23/kWh), locks the connector, and starts the energy offer.

------------------------------------------------------------
TEST CASE 12
Title: Failure Postcondition - Local Authorization List not accepted due to misconfiguration

Scenario:
The Local Authorization List is unavailable (LocalAuthListAvailable = false) or
improperly configured, causing the Charging Station to be unable to accept tokens via
the local list.

Steps:
1. Set LocalAuthListAvailable to false or otherwise make the Local Authorization List
   unavailable.
2. Add an IdToken to the Local Authorization List data structure with status Accepted
   (even though the feature is reported as unavailable).
3. Bring the Charging Station online.
4. Have the EV Driver present that IdToken.
5. Observe the Charging Station behavior: does it use the list or fall back to the CSMS?

Expected Result:
Because the Local Authorization List is not available, the Charging Station does not use
it to authorize the token. It either falls back to the CSMS or rejects the token if no
fallback is possible. The failure postcondition (Charging Station does not accept tokens
on the Local Authorization List) is confirmed.
